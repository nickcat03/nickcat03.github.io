Now you get to know the Niplore

Wow, so you're that interested and want to know a thing or two about me?

Well, before we discuss about me, I want to make sure you're doing alright.

In order to listen to the stories of others, and to learn something from them, we need to find ourselves first. What is your "About You"?

Once you've taken a moment to think about that, let yourself know that your story is important, and that you have the ability to share that too. People care about your story, and people will listen to you.

Anyways, about me... I'm a 19 y/o Cybersecurity major who lives in New York City. I'm a big tech nerd, I think that's the best way I could explain myself. I'm obsessed with computers and electronics, I cannot imagine a world without them. I sound kinda generic though, don't I? I'm pretty sure any person in this day and age is not able to function without technology... which, when you think about it, it's kind of scary. Yes, we have relied on man-made objects such as homes, streets, cars, planes, etc. But computers are different, they are constantly changing and growing.

Compare a car now to a car from 50 years ago. What do they have in common? Well, for one, they both drive. You can use a car from 50 years ago today. You'll miss the air conditioning though, I wouldn't recommend it. The point is, you can still use that car today, and it functions as a car.

Now compare a computer from 50 years ago to computers we have now. Do you think you'd be able to use it today? I don't think so. It is a computer, I'm not denying that. But the world changed so much that those past computers are now basically useless. We are still able to use older cars because our physical landscape has not changed enough for them to become obsolete, but our digital landscape is always changing, and these computers are not fast enough to keep up with what we need.

So now you're probably asking yourself, "why is this weirdo getting all technical in his About Me page?" and that's because this is what I think about. I'm always thinking about how technology has changed, how it has affected the world, how it was being used 10, 20, 30 years ago, as well as the inevitable (and scary) future. It's very fascinating.

I wanted to avoid the generic garbage that no one cares about. I want people to learn something from my own experiences, and I think that is the best "About Me" I could ask for.
